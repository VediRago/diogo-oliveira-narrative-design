# Project: Interactive Narrative Experiments

## Overview

This section tracks small experiments in interactive storytelling, pacing, reader/player perception, and narrative presentation.

The goal is to explore how story can be shaped through interaction, timing, layout, and controlled information flow.

---

## Current Focus

Current experiments are focused on:

- Twine presentation
- cinematic text timing
- layered images
- emotional pacing
- reader guidance
- non-choice-based interactive storytelling
- visual storytelling inside simple web formats

---

## Design Questions

These experiments ask:

- How can a static story feel interactive without relying on branching choices?
- How can timing change the emotional meaning of text and images?
- How much information should be shown before the reader understands the context?
- How can visual rhythm support narrative recognition?

---

- Twine
