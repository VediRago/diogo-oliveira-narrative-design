# Layer 1 — Regulatus

## Core Truth

Regulatus is no longer simply a government.

Regulatus is civilization.

After more than two millennia, law, trade, administration, citizenship, and daily life are built upon it. Very few people wish to destroy Regulatus. Most people cannot imagine a world without it.

## Modern Conflict

The conflict is not Regulatus versus anti-Regulatus.

The conflict is over what Regulatus means.

Every major faction claims to defend the Way. Every major faction believes someone else betrayed it.

## Citizen View

Most citizens believe the state has failed the Way. They do not believe the Way itself is wrong.

Common belief:

> The Way remains. The walkers changed.

## Gang Interpretation

Many gangs believe they are closer to the original ideals of Regulatus than the administrators.

Their argument is simple: they protect their district, feed their people, punish predators, and maintain local order. The state abandoned these duties.

## Novacula Interpretation

Novacula argues that Regulatus was corrupted.

Not disproven.

Not destroyed.

Corrupted.

## Virtus Interpretation

Virtus believes Regulatus survives through strength. Weakness is the greatest threat to civilization.

## Lux Interpretation

Lux believes Regulatus without compassion is incomplete. The state preserved the structure but lost part of its soul.

## North Interpretation

The North respects the ideals of law and order. Its criticism is that rulers must be bound by the same principles they demand from others.

## Era Four Status

By 2600 AVD, almost every faction claims to defend Regulatus.

The argument is no longer about belief.

The argument is about interpretation.
