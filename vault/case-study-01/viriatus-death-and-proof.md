# Viriatus — Death and Proof

## Purpose

Explains how Viriatus's death became usable as proof of Ventari discipline, deepening the "Proof" step of the calendar-origin chain.

## Core Principle

A remembered figure isn't enough to found a civilization's calendar on. His life had to be turned into evidence for a specific claim, or House Ventari's new order would begin as visible betrayal instead of preservation.

## Explanation

Viriatus dies at the exact point his private identity and public meaning collide — privately grieving his adoptive father Ecalus as a son, publicly already becoming the obvious figure for a reshaped order. That collision is what makes his death both useful and cruel: if the public knew he was murdered while mourning, the new order starts as accusation. If the public believes he died nobly in war, the new order starts as preservation.

Ermerus doesn't hold the blade. His role is interpretation — turning Ilunis's private jealousy and humiliation into "family duty," then turning public grief into doctrine. Viriatus becomes proof that Ventari discipline succeeded where the old order failed: his victories get used retroactively as evidence for a claim about the Ventari bloodline, not just a fact about Viriatus himself.

That's the mechanism the "Proof" step in the case study compresses: Viriatus's death only becomes politically usable once it stops being *his* story and starts being evidence for *someone else's* claim.
