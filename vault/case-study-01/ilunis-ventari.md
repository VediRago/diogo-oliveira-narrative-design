# Ilunis Ventari

Ilunis Ventari is the second son of House Ventari.

Where Rutare Ventari is protected because he is heir, Ilunis is overused because he is useful. He is strong, sent into danger, and treated as someone who can survive what others cannot.

## The Maiming

At the Battle of Augunar, Ilunis is maimed while defending Ventari lands from invaders. His lower body is permanently damaged, leaving him barely able to walk. The injury takes his mobility and the proof of worth House Ventari taught him to rely on.

Ilunis believes he gave his body to the family and still lost his place beside Ecalus Ventari. Viriatus becomes the living proof that sacrifice did not earn Ilunis what he wanted.

To Ilunis, the wound is simple:

> I gave my body to this family, and Father gave his pride to someone else.

## The Logic He Builds

After Augunar, Ilunis learns the wrong lesson. Rather than blame the family that overused him, he decides mercy and softness failed him. If pain was necessary, then his suffering was not meaningless. If force is order, then his damaged body can still belong to strength.

This protects his loyalty to House Ventari while giving him something to hate. It also means his later cruelty can feel like duty instead of revenge.

Mercy becomes an enemy idea. Softness becomes weakness. Force becomes the only language worth speaking.

## Ermerus's Influence

After Ecalus Ventari dies, Ermerus Ventari gives Ilunis's jealousy the shape of duty. The idea is simple and dangerous:

> Viriatus is not their blood.

That thought lets Ilunis see the crime as protection of House Ventari rather than admission of wounded envy. He can tell himself he is protecting the family instead of confessing he is broken by his father's affection for an outsider.

## The Murder

During family mourning, while Viriatus grieves Ecalus Ventari as a son, Ilunis commits the private betrayal. The murder is not battlefield glory. It is a private stabbing of someone vulnerable and grieving.

What the murder gives Ilunis matters. It restores agency. After years of watching his body fail him, killing Viriatus makes him feel decisive and strong again. To Ilunis, it feels like reclaiming what he is owed.

## Later Consequence

The Ilunis line becomes tied to law, punishment, judgment, military authority, and sanctioned violence. Their descendants may control courts, military command, and public punishment. Their power is visible and feared, but carries a contradiction from its origin.

They punish betrayal while descending from betrayal.

This means they must make force righteous. They justify severity as protection, judgment as stability, and punishment as duty. They turn being used into a doctrine of necessary sacrifice, and that doctrine becomes institutional.
