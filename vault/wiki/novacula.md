# Novacula

Novacula begins from rejection inside the same house that created [Regulatus](regulatus.md).

After [Ermerus Ventari](ermerus-ventari.md) dies, his disputed children lose protection inside [House Ventari](house-ventari.md). Later family branches use blood legitimacy to remove them and their claim from secure power.

That rejection becomes the wound Novacula grows from.

Novacula does not begin as a simple rejection of meaning. It begins as a rejection of a meaning controlled by the people who cast them out.

This makes Novacula one of the most dangerous consequences of the founding era. They are not outsiders who misunderstand Regulatus. They are born from the bloodline of the man who shaped its public meaning, then discarded by the legitimacy doctrine he helped weaponize.

In that sense, Ermerus indirectly creates the blade that cuts back at his own work. His children are outcast by the system he helped build, and their descendants inherit both the wound and the knowledge of how the system thinks.

Where Regulatus turns [Viriatus](viriatus.md)'s death into order, law, and public truth, Novacula turns exclusion into suspicion, rebellion, and the need to cut through deception.
