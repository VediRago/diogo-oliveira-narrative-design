# Repository Principles

The principles governing the narrative design methods documented in this repository.

Shared structure — README, Case Studies, Supporting Files, Vault, and the rules governing how they relate — is defined once in the Layer 2 Blueprint and applies here without needing to be restated. What follows is specific to narrative design as a discipline.

## Story First, Systems After

Rules should not trap the story before its emotional shape exists.

Systems get built by noticing what the story already keeps returning to, then asking why those details matter — not by inventing structure in advance and forcing the story to fit it. If a rule ever starts shaping the story instead of the story shaping the rule, the rule is wrong, not the story.

## Cause Needs Consequence

An unusual idea needs a foundation. A power needs a limit. An exception needs a cost.

If a detail exists only because it sounds interesting, it becomes a weak patch — it may solve one scene, but it damages immersion, because the audience begins to feel that anything can happen whenever the story needs it. Every claim about a character, place, institution, or belief should be traceable to the pressure that produced it.

## The Causal Chain

The working method:

```text
experience
↓
emotion
↓
reason
↓
behavior
```

This extends when the situation demands it:

```text
→ atmosphere
→ history
→ quest logic
```

The chain applies at every scale — a character, a city, a faction, an NPC, a quest — without needing a different formula at each size. Only the depth changes.

## The Extension Rule

Do not extend the chain unless the next layer is earned by everything before it.

Each added layer works as a constraint, not decoration — it narrows which possibilities are still valid, rather than adding more of them. A character might only need experience → behavior. An institution might need the full chain to history. The test for whether a layer belongs: could this step be removed without the next one losing its reason for existing? If yes, it hasn't been earned yet.

## Separate Files When Ideas Branch

When an idea grows into its own concern, it gets its own file instead of being forced into a document that no longer answers one question.

This keeps each document focused on a single question while preserving the causal chain behind every idea.
